KATIE: Research Questions

  1. To what extent do SGMY report bystander intentions in situations of TDV and RAU?

  2. What are the associations between TDV bystander intentions relate to RAU bystander intentions?

    1. demographic/social positioning variables (i.e., age, gender, sex assigned at birth, sexual orientation, and race/ethnicity),

    2. TDV victimization and perpetration, alcohol use, perceptions of social norms specific to alcohol use and TDV, depression, emotional dysregulation, and population-specific factors (e.g., internalized homo/bi/trans phobia, outness, and LGBTQ+ sense of community). 

IVs: BYpru & BYreu & LTI

Interested Variables:

  1. age

  2. gender

  3. sex-at-birth

  4. SexOri

  5. race

  6. TDV variable

  7. ACOMu - Alcohol consequences

  8. perceptions of social norms specific to alcohol use and tdv

  9. depression

  10. emotinoal dysregulation

  11. internalized negativity/phobia

  12. outness

RQ3

  1. To what extent do TDV and RAU bystander intentions relate to demographic/social positioning variables (i.e., age, gender, sex assigned at birth, sexual orientation, and race/ethnicity), TDV victimization and perpetration, alcohol use, perceptions of social norms specific to alcohol use and TDV, depression, emotional dysregulation, and population-specific factors (e.g., internalized homo/bi/trans phobia, outness, and LGBTQ+ sense of community. 

A. Intervening Intentions (3x)

  1. TDV Bystander Intentions, Reactive (Y1BYreu)

  2. TDV Bystander Intentions, Proactive (Y1BYpru)

  3. Alcohol Intervene (Y1LTIm)

B. Demographic/social positioning variables (6x)

  1. age (y0age)

  2. gender, Cis-girl v. Cis-boy (cis_boy)

  3. gender, Cis-girl v. TGD (tgd)

  4. sex assigned at birth (y1sex)

  5. sexual orientation (sexori_group)

  6. race/ethnicity (y1racc)

C. Health Outcome Variables (5x)

  1. TDV Victimizatoin (y1TDVMd)

  2. TDV Perpetration (y1TDPMd)

  3. [Risky] Alcohol Use (Y1ACOMu)

  4. Social Norms, Alcohol Use (y1dno1Mr)

  5. Social Norms, TDV (y1DanM)

D. Population-specific Factors (4x)

  1. Internalized homo/bi/trans phobia (Y1HMNm)

  2. Outness, Sexual Identity (Y1OSOd)

  3. Outness, Gender Identity (Y1OGId)

  4. LGBT+ Sense of Community (Y1COMm)

(i.e., age, gender, sex assigned at birth, sexual orientation, and race/ethnicity), TDV victimization and perpetration, alcohol use, perceptions of social norms specific to alcohol use and TDV, depression, emotional dysregulation, and population-specific factors (e.g., internalized homo/bi/trans phobia, outness, and LGBTQ+ sense of community. 

To answer these questions we have performed linear regression model to include all variables (continuous and categorical). Variables with >3 categories had multiple dummy code variables (Such that if it had three categories, there would be two sub-variabels [0,1; 0,1] with 0 as the reference group).

result <- rcorr(as.matrix(bys_data))

# Assuming 'result' is the output from the rcorr function
cor_matrix <- result$r  # Correlation coefficients
p_matrix <- result$P    # P-values

# Initialize an empty matrix with the same dimensions as cor_matrix and p_matrix
combined_matrix <- matrix(nrow = nrow(cor_matrix), ncol = ncol(cor_matrix))

# Populate the combined matrix with formatted strings
for (i in 1:nrow(cor_matrix)) {
  for (j in 1:ncol(cor_matrix)) {
    combined_matrix[i, j] <- paste0("r = ", round(cor_matrix[i, j], 3),
                                    ", p = ", formatC(p_matrix[i, j], format = "e", digits = 2))
  }
}
